Creating ADHD-Friendly Information Separation

Introduction

Organizing information in an ADHD-friendly way involves clear separation, visual hierarchy, and concise presentation. Let's explore these principles.

Organization

Group related information together. Use clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points to break down content into easily digestible chunks.

  • Group similar topics
  • Use descriptive headings
  • Employ bullet points for lists

Visual Hierarchy

Emphasize important information through visual cues. Use larger font sizes, bold text, and colors to create a clear hierarchy of information.

  • Highlight key points
  • Use a consistent color scheme
  • Make important links noticeable

Conciseness

Avoid information overload. Be concise and to the point. Eliminate unnecessary details and provide summaries where possible.

  • Keep paragraphs short
  • Provide summaries or key takeaways
  • Avoid unnecessary details
